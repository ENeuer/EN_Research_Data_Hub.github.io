Information Architecture Analysis

Discovering how information is being handled within Homeday

Challenge

Understand how, when and by whom information is being collected, transformed, stored and being passed on to offer customers the best experience

Role / Responsibilities

Task Force Member, Lead & Support

  • Derive interview guidelines for internal interviews
  • Interview conduction & results analysis
  • Workshop preparation & conduction
  • Result presentation in form of a narrative

Collaborators

CPO, Engineering Lead/Founder, Lead Project Manager, Design Lead, Research Lead, BI

Description of the image

Description of the image

Approach & Method

  1. Internal interviews to discover pain points and current information flow
  2. Mapping of information flow and inventoriy analysis
  3. Affinity mapping of discovered pain points
  4. Inhouse workshops with interviewees to verify discovered pains, break up silos and collaboratively develop a revised information flow

Outcome Examples

  • Processes connected to the information architecture are not defined properly
  • The technocological software isn't optimal for information handling leading to teams developing workarounds. Especially the user interface of the software is hindering efficient work
  • There is no clear ownership for certain information
  • Knowledge silos exist between the internal teams of the companies. There are big gaps on understanding what the other teams are working on as well as their responsibilities
