Introducing Continuous Discovery

Shifting towards an opportunity-centered product approach

Challenge

  • Majority of research is project-based & validation-focused
  • Insights don't provide a holistic view of the entire customer journey
  • User needs and pain points aren't fully understood, potentially leading to ineffective problem solving

Goal

  • Kick-start a new researching habit within the product team by continuosly engaging with customers
  • Discover (unknown) opportunities, supporting informed decisions on feature prioritization
  • Resolve tensions between business and customer needs by providing ongoing user feedback and data

Exemplary process of Continuous Discovery

Role / Responsibilities

Leading UX Researcher & Strategic Initiator

  • Development of process addressing current needs and maturity of product team
  • Pitch of benefits and process to department head
  • Secured buy-in and funding
  • Conduction of research project

Collaborators

Product Owner of end-to-end customer journey team, UX Designer

Approach & Method

  • Bi-weekly interviews with targeted customer group using the Jobs-to-Be-Done Framework
  • Involvement of stakeholders in interviewing process
  • Categorisation of identified opportunities into the opportunity-solution tree
  • Prioritisation workshop with product owners responsible for individual journey step

Exemplary Opportunity-Solution Tree inspired by Teresa Torres

Reflections & Learnings

  • The implementation of Continuos Research takes time and must fit the team's capabilities and product maturity.
  • Every team's journey looks different. A first step can be as simple as conducting holistic interviews on a regular basis.
  • Engagement, understanding and (financial) buy-in must be given for successful transition. Make use of pilot projects to show-case benefits.
  • Be careful to not slack on quality of research, such as documentation, usage of multiple methods and sources, etc.
